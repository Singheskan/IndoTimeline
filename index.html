Timeline of my Semester in Indonesia

Arrival

The flight was long, really long, 24 hours from Frankfurt to Jakarta with an eight hour stop in Doha. I had a lot of time checking out the airport which was easily explorable through a futuristic, mostly glass-made railway driving above the heads of mere citizens which were eager to find their gates by foot. After arriving in Jakarta and passing the border control I was greeted by students of the Universitas Indonesia (UI) dressed in bright, yellow suit jackets (looking as bad as it sounds). Hasbi was one of them which accompanied me and William, another student from Darmstadt, to our apartments at the Margonda Residence in Depok. As we were on the road around 5pm we were not the only ones trying to get their way to Jakarta. The traffic was unbelievable and scooter drivers insane in their confidence to maneuver through this see of vehicles. It was intense but we made it out alive as it seems to be a general practice of Indonesians to drive in such circumstances. My buddy Desti welcomed me at the residence and showed me my apartment in the allegedly 16th floor of the building. (The elevator misses out floor 14 and 15 as they’re not selectable) Afterwards, I could catch a good night of sleep, even though an Islamic priest woke me up at 4 in the morning. It was a good day.

12.08.19

Traffic

The Imam woke me up again. I used my free sleepless time to find out how to embed videos. The traffic is really insane!

13.08.19

Orientation

It is crazy how living in a completely different country can become simple routine in just a week. The crowded shattering of a docent vehicles fades to white noise while I manage to find my way through Depok (city on the southern border of Jakarta) by myself. My co-students and I underwent several events in the last days, including orientation days to get to know the campus and the library (which is apparently the biggest in south-east Asia), as well as team building events to get to know each other. Surprisingly, everyone seems to get along independent of cultural, religious, or national differences. The Indonesian people are so proactive and helpful that anxieties of any kind are never present. Another student was capturing this feeling very good by saying that: “I am so cherished and cared for that I do not feel as I have left home.” One of the events was the Orientation Day in which we were introduced into some organizational topics as well as cultural aspects of Indonesia, including a local dance performed bare foot with a plate in each hand. Those plates were circularly moved while dancing and sometimes even moved on without breaking. I have never seen something that exotic before. In the last days I had also enough time to organize my apartment, buy a second hand guitar in Jakarta and spend a whole day in Jakarta’s tourist districts. As it seems, we tourists are part of the attractions as well, especially the Caucasian looking exchange students, as several inhabitants asked them frequently to take pictures with them. Even I was asked twice! Today I had the chance to meet Dr. Ade Azurat a member of the faculty of computer science at UI who gave me a tour through the campus and even introduced me to everyone I might contact in the future, including the head of Master students, secretariat, and faculty speaker. He is a very nice guy with a great taste of humor. I am planning to take a Bioinformatics and Advanced Computer Vision class. As I am working as a freelancer for Fraunhofer I will probably not find the time to take more courses. (Besides seeing the country of course)

19.08.19

Culture & Nature

The area around Jakarta is filled with various mysterious places and stores in which you can easily loose yourself. One of them was a miniature park representing different areas of Indonesia, including Bali, Sumatra, and Borneo. The UI Buddy Club organized a tour for us in which we were introduced to the cultural differences between the different parts of Indonesia. Those differences were shown in examples of ceremonial dresses, tools & weaponry, as well as construction and art. Even though the regions uniformed as one country they are still remarkably unique and differentiable from each other in every way, even in language. Though there exists an official language, Bahasa Indonesia (literally translated to “Language Indonesia”), every region speaks their own language as well. Bahasa is more of a stripped down to the basic language used for communication with the Dutch heirs in the early days. Beside of cultural insights I had finally some time to spend in nature! Bogor, a city located under Depok, hosts a beautiful botanical garden as well as a resident of the Indonesian president. It is filled with various in Latin-labeled plants and trees of which my roommate could have made more sense of than me (as he focusses on Plant Biology). Nevertheless, I could still admire the beauty of it to the fullest.

26.08.19

1000 Islands

Pulau Seribu (“1000 Islands”) is a chain of actually around 110 islands located north-west of Jakarta. We were staying for three days at Pramuka Island and exploring the surrounding islands from there including the National Marine Park, a sanctuary for Hawksbill Sea Turtles and local sharks. You can find the location of our stay in the attached Google Map. (I can only use the Google API cost-free in developer mode, sorry for the inconvenience) The locals’ life a lay-back life fishing, dancing, and singing all day and do not worry too much about the hectic life of their fellow citizens in Jakarta. We had a chance to calm down and were indulging paradise for a little bit. I saw some kids fighting like in a series I watched as a kid called “Naruto”. Somehow that made me cringe but also nostalgic at the same time.

29.08.19

First Day

Today is my first day of university and my first course, Advanced Image Processing hold by Prof. Aniati a very slow-paced, old women which was involved in several research topics including Change Recognition for landscapes, Sleep Analysis, and Cancer Detection. Really interesting stuff, even though she were not really able to go deeper into the topics. Nevertheless, she still managed to end her lecture in 45 minutes. Even the lecturers are laid-back here. As it turns out, Bioinformatics is not available this semester which is why I switched to Advanced Machine Learning instead. The master students have a dedicated room for studying, likewise the bachelor and degree students. Luckily, I was able to make some new friends. I was really glad as I am the only exchange student in computer science this semester. I was just sitting down for a few hours and already had a few helping hands for my upcoming Bahasa Indonesia class or any other obstacles as well as a few new numbers to call.
